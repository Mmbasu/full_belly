Food Waste and Food Insecurity Statistics

World Food Program

Up to 50% of food produced in Kenya goes to waste every year. Women and children are particularly affected by food insecurity in Kenya. Over 2.6 million people in Kenya are acutely food insecure.

World Bank

Over 20% of the population is estimated to be undernourished in Kenya Food loss and waste in Kenya cost the country approximately KES 150 billion (around US $1.4 billion) annually Kenya's economy could grow by up to 3.4% if food waste was reduced by 50% Reducing food waste and losses in Kenya could have significant economic benefits, including cost savings for farmers and businesses, and increased food availability for consumers.

Mission Statement

The Full Belly Project is dedicated to fighting food waste and insecurity in Kenya We aim to address the humanitarian, environmental, and economic consequences of food waste and insecurity. Our web application connects food service providers with local charities and organizations to reduce food waste and fight hunger in a sustainable and impactful way.

Vision Statement

Our vision at the Full Belly Project is to create a world where every individual has access to nutritious food, and where food waste is minimized.
